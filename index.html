Adopting Rocks!

Give a pet a second chance!

Pet adoption is the process of transferring responsibility for a pet that was previously owned by another party such as a person, shelter, or rescue organization. Common sources for adoptable pets are animal shelters and rescue groups. Some organizations give adopters ownership of the pet, while others use a guardianship model wherein the organization retains some control over the animal's future use or care.

My current adopted pets

Tigrito

He was born in June 2019 under my parent's house. My family used to feed his mom, who gave birth to 5 kittens. We were able to give 3 in adoption, but Tigrito and his sister, Negrita, were not adopted. Negrita is a very independent and outgoing cat, she loves to hunt and the freedom of living outside. Tigrito was the total opposite. He loved to be with us and he was very comfortable inside the house. We found out my stepdad was allergic to cats, so Tigrito was not allowed to enter the house anymore. Eventually, when I moved out, I brought him with me. Now he is 3 years old.

Itto

My husband's family found him wondering around the neighborhood after Hurricane Matthew hit Miami in 2016. They tried to find the owners, but no one claimed him. The family got attached to his sweet personality, especially my husband, and they decided to adopt him. Eventually, when my husband and I got married, we decided to bring Itto with us. He is currently 8 years old.
